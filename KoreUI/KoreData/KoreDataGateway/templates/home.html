plato OR aristotle Either side may match Useful for alternate names, rival schools, or multiple candidate topics
plato | aristotle Same as OR, using a shorter shorthand Quick way to broaden a search without typing the full operator
stoic NOT roman Results must contain stoic and must not contain roman Useful for removing a noisy term that dominates results
(plato OR socrates) dialogue Parentheses group alternatives before combining with another required term Finds dialogue material about either Plato or Socrates
roman, republic Comma still behaves like the old separator, so both terms are required Keeps earlier searches working without changing meaning

Quick rules: bare terms default to AND, OR or | broadens a search, NOT excludes a term, and parentheses control grouping. Wrap any multi-word phrase in double quotes to require exact adjacency.

The boolean syntax above applies to the text-search services: Feeds, Reference, Library, RAG, and Scrape. Graph is still term-expansion based, so complex boolean operators are mainly useful for the other five services. The Since / Until date filters apply to feeds only. The Limit value is applied per selected service. The default is 20, but you can raise it as high as 200 when you need broader retrieval.
